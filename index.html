PERSONAL

Sodi Kroehler is a senior at the University of Pittsburgh, majoring in computer science and a minoring in philosophy. After growing up in and then leaving the religious group known as the Twelve Tribes in order to pursue an education, Sodi has now become a terrible artist, pictured left.

He is hoping to continue his education as PhD student next year, focusing on the interplay between ethics and AI applications. He is especially excited to work on misinformation-reduction technologies.

Outside of school, he is involved in several school organizations, such as the Computer Science Club and the Alpha Kappa Psi professional fraternity. He also serves as the technical director for Sudan Rebirth Ministry, a charity focused on providing academic scholarships for children in South Sudan.

He is also an avid writer, having recently published his first feature-length book, “Homo Stultus - for gods”.
